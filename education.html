Simple Ways to Reduce Food Waste

Why Fight Food Waste?

One-third of all food produced is wasted globally. That's 1.3 billion tons per year!

In the Kitchen
Smart Shopping
PROBLEM: Forgotten leftovers that get thrown away
SOLUTION: Create a "Eat Me First" container in your fridge. Label containers with dates. Turn leftovers into new meals like stir-fries or soups.
PROBLEM: Fruits and vegetables go bad quickly
SOLUTION: Store properly! Keep fruits and vegetables separate. Use breathable bags for vegetables. Freeze fruits for smoothies before they spoil.
PROBLEM: Buying too much food that expires
SOLUTION: Make a meal plan before shopping. Check what you already have. Make a list and stick to it. Buy smaller amounts more frequently.
PROBLEM: Confusion about food date labels
SOLUTION: "Best before" means quality, not safety. "Use by" is about safety. Trust your senses - look, smell, and taste (if safe) to check if food is still good.
PROBLEM: Food scraps going to landfill
SOLUTION: Compost food scraps to create nutrient-rich soil for plants. Start with a simple backyard bin or countertop collector.
